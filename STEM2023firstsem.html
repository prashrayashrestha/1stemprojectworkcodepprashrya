SALT

The ionic interaction between sodium (Na) and chlorine (Cl) creates salt, commonly known as sodium chloride (NaCl). Na+ and Cl- ions are created when sodium gives one electron to chlorine in the atomic structure of salt. Because of their opposing charges, the positively charged sodium ion (Na+) and the negatively charged chloride ion (Cl-) are drawn to one another and create a powerful ionic connection. The sodium ion attracts the chlorine ion by the force of attraction between opposing charges, forming an electrostatic connection between the two elements. The high melting and boiling temperatures of salt, as well as its capacity to conduct electricity when dissolved, are all a result of this ionic connection.

ETHANOL

Ethanol, often known as ethyl alcohol, grain alcohol, drinking alcohol, or just alcohol, is an organic substance (abbreviated as EtOH). With the chemical formula C2H6O, it is an alcohol. The formula for this substance is C2H5OH (an ethyl group joined to a hydroxyl group), CH3CH2OH, or C2H5OH. A volatile, flammable, colorless liquid known as ethanol has a distinctive wine-like odor and bitter flavorIt is a euphoric recreational substance that serves as the primary component of alcoholic beverages. Natural sources of ethanol include the fermentation of carbohydrates by yeasts or petrochemical processes like ethylene hydration. It has current medicinal uses as an antiseptic, disinfectant, solvent for certain drugs, and an antidote for methanol and ethylene glycol toxicity in addition to its historical usage as a general anesthetic.
